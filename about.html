Our Story

It started with 50 bottles of Popia Snack 🥟

It was the lead-up to Hari Raya Aidilfitri — one of the most joyful festive seasons, where families gather, share food, and celebrate together. Like so many home-based sellers during this time, I had an idea: make something special and sell it.

So I made Popia Snack, bottled up and ready to sell. I had a small starting capital — just enough to buy ingredients and packaging for 50 bottles. Nothing fancy. Just a dream to earn a little extra income doing something I loved.

I had the product. I had the passion. But I had absolutely no idea if I was actually making a profit.

The Excel Sheet Struggle 📋

Like most small sellers, I turned to Excel. I opened a blank spreadsheet and started keying in my ingredients — spring roll pastry, filling, oil, seasoning, bottles, labels, packaging tape.

But problems piled up fast. How much did I actually use per bottle? What was my real cost per unit? Was my selling price giving me a healthy margin — or was I barely breaking even?

I kept second-guessing myself. I'd calculate something, then realise I forgot the packaging. Then I'd redo it. Then I'd forget the gas, or the time I spent cooking. It was slow. It was messy. And after all that effort, I still wasn't confident my numbers were right.

I was guessing more than I was actually calculating. And that's a dangerous way to run even a small business.

The moment I decided to build something 💡

Sitting there with that Excel sheet open — ingredients spread across the kitchen table, Hari Raya songs playing in the background — one thought hit me clearly:

There must be a better way for people like me.

Not complicated accounting software. Not a full business dashboard with charts nobody understands. Just a simple, fast, free tool where you enter your ingredients, costs, and quantity — and it instantly tells you your cost per unit, what price to sell at, and exactly how much profit you'll make.

Something built specifically for home sellers, market vendors, Hari Raya side hustlers, and first-time small business owners who are starting small and just need clear numbers — not a finance degree.
